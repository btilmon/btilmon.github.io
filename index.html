Brevin Tilmon

Hello, I'm Brevin Tilmon. I'm currently a Senior Software Engineer at Quidient. Before Quidient, I received my PhD in computer vision from the Florida Optics and Computational Sensor Lab at the University of Florida where I was advised by Sanjeev J. Koppal. My core skillset is in high performance software and applied research for computer graphics, computer vision, and machine learning, but I also have hardware experience. On the hardware side, I have built a few custom imaging systems (new kinds of cameras and depth sensors) with custom synchronization circuits and embedded software techniques such as interfacing with peripherals, microcontroller programming, and concurrency. On top of the hardware was usually custom optimized GPU and CPU code running computer vision and machine learning algorithms, which is what eventually pulled me more to the software side of things.

Notable past experiences (research scientist internships during my PhD) include building a SOTA software-defined depth sensor at Snap Research in the Computational Imaging Team for their Spectacles augmented reality glasses, working on efficient machine learning models for computer vision at Meta, and building a computational imaging simulator with CUDA/NVIDIA OptiX for 3D reconstruction and reflectance estimation at NASA Intelligent Robotics Group. I have also published computer vision papers in CVPR, ICCV, PAMI, 3DV, ICCP, and did real time demos of hardware prototypes at conferences. I have also created and contributed to a few open source projects. A few of my favorite ever-changing open source projects are Ingonyama, tinygrad, and llama.cpp.

Some notable things I do in my free time include hardware hacking, tweaking SOTA machine learning models for fun applications, dirt biking, and reading (textbooks, scifi, history). I am currently based out of Columbia, Maryland. Feel free to reach me at brevinjt@gmail.com, and here is my GitHub and Google Scholar.
